Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about our live, online language courses.

How do the courses work?
Our core format is a 10-week live course on Zoom, meeting twice per week for 1 hour per session. Classes are interactive, with speaking practice, guided activities, and feedback from your tutor.
What levels do you offer and how do I find my level?
We offer multiple levels (A1→A2, A2→B1, B1→B2). New learners complete a short pre-course quiz to place you accurately. If you’re taking French, see French placement quiz. For Spanish, use Spanish placement quiz.
What are the class sizes?
We keep classes small for quality interaction. Typical groups are 5–12 students. If fewer than 5 students enroll, we may postpone or offer alternatives (e.g., moving to the next start date or private options).
What do I need to join the classes?
A computer or tablet, a stable internet connection, a microphone, and the free Zoom app. Headphones are recommended for clearer audio. We’ll share any extra tools or links in your course welcome email.
What if I miss a class?
We don’t reschedule individual sessions, but we’ll help you catch up with lesson notes, assignments, and tutor guidance via email. Reach out to your tutor if you have questions on missed material.
How does pricing and payment work?
Course pricing is posted on each course page during enrollment. We accept major credit/debit cards. If you need an invoice or employer reimbursement documentation, we’re happy to provide it.
Do you offer any discounts or referrals?
We periodically run promotions and a bring-a-friend referral offer (e.g., both students save on tuition). Check the enrollment page for the current discount and eligibility details.
What materials are included?
You’ll receive digital materials (slides, practice activities, and weekly assignments). Your tutor may also recommend free online resources for additional practice between sessions.
Do you provide a certificate of completion?
Yes. Upon successful completion of the course requirements, we can provide a digital certificate noting the level and hours completed.
Are the courses for adults only, or can teens join?
We welcome learners of many ages. Some cohorts are adult-focused, while teen-friendly options are available. If you’re enrolling a teen, let us know so we can place them in the best cohort.
Which time zone do you use? Do you serve international students?
Class times are listed in Eastern Time (ET), and we welcome learners worldwide. If you’re outside ET, we recommend adding sessions to your calendar to auto-convert to your local time.
What is your cancellation or withdrawal policy?
Our policy is outlined in the Student Agreement you accept at enrollment. If you need to withdraw, email us as soon as possible and we’ll review options per the current policy.
Do you provide accessibility accommodations?
Yes. We’re committed to accessible learning. Let us know your needs (e.g., captions, extra processing time, materials in advance), and we’ll work with you to provide reasonable accommodations.
